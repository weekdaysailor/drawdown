Strategic Coordination Lab · Scenario file 1.5° · Two-price console

DRAWDOWN // SOURCE & SINK

Two instruments, two axes. One prices the carbon you emit; one pays for the carbon you remove. Find out what each can — and can't — do.

▸ Console briefing

Five blocs share one atmosphere. Each cycle a bloc makes two separate decisions: how to power itself — burn dirty (cheap, emits) or abate (costlier, clean) — and whether to remove carbon from the air. Left alone, every bloc burns and none removes: the commons cooks. Run it once and watch.

You hold two instruments, and they act on different decisions:

  • TAX CLUB · ETS + border tariff — source-side. Prices emissions, so burning stops paying. A border tariff coerces outsiders in. But a price can only push emissions toward zero — it can't pull carbon back out, and taxes hurt someone: push the price too high and blocs revolt, hollow it out, and keep burning.
  • CARBON REWARD · GCR — sink-side, and more. A performance reward for climate-mitigating activities — carbon removal, ecosystem restoration, and emissions reductions — paid to whoever delivers them. Here it does two things: it funds removal (the only path below zero), and it finances the clean switch — but a subsidy pays for a replacement, it doesn’t make the old plant unprofitable. With no carbon price, the coal plant is still cheap to run, so it lingers and the subsidized clean capacity just adds alongside it (Jevons). The reward retires the plant only when a price is also on it. It can reward, never penalize — so it can’t move a revenue-locked petrostate, and it’s capped by the CQE / inflation ceiling. It also brings in-country investment that raises the political tolerance for a carbon price.

Run each alone, then together. Watch where each one stalls — and why neither finishes the job by itself.

One caveat up front: the temperature here responds within a few cycles so you can see cause and effect. The real climate doesn't. Even after the world goes net-negative, ocean and thermal inertia mean warming keeps coasting upward for decades before it actually turns down. The direction of each instrument's effect is the lesson; the speed is compressed for clarity.
